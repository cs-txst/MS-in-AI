# Degree & Graduation Requirements

---

This document outlines the official academic requirements that must be met by all students to earn the Master of Science in Artificial Intelligence degree. This page is intended for admitted and current students. For information on how to apply, please see the [Admissions Information](./ADMISSIONS.md) page.

### Core Degree Requirements

1.  **Credit Hours:** All students must successfully complete **30 graduate credit hours**.
2.  **Course Structure:** The 30 hours must conform to the program structure of 4 required core courses, 4 AI-related electives, and 2 open electives. For a full list of courses, see the [Curriculum Details](./CURRICULUM.md) page.
3.  **Academic Standing:** A grade of "C" or better must be earned in all courses, and students must maintain good academic standing as defined by The Graduate College.

### Background Requirements

Students admitted through the [Pathways Program](./PATHWAYS-PROGRAM.md) must complete all required foundational coursework with a grade of "C" or better before enrolling in graduate courses.

### Comprehensive Examination Requirements

The comprehensive exam for the MS in AI program consists of multiple components that must be completed to be eligible for graduation.

1.  **Degree Outline:** A formal degree outline must be prepared and approved before the end of your first semester. This is typically completed during the mandatory diagnostic interview with the graduate advisor upon admission.

2.  **Programming Exam:** All students must pass a written programming exam to demonstrate their ability to solve problems and write clear, logical code.
    *   The exam is typically administered the week before the Fall and Spring semesters begin.
    *   Students who do not pass the exam are required to enroll in and pass the remedy course, **CS 5301: Programming Practicum**, with a grade of "C" or better.

3.  **Communication Exam:** All students must demonstrate proficiency in technical writing. This can be satisfied by **one** of the following options:
    *   Having an official score of 3.5 or higher on the Analytical Writing section of the Graduate Record Examination (GRE).
    *   Taking and passing the departmental Communication Exam, which is offered once during your first long semester.

4.  **Seminar Attendance:** All students are required to attend at least **four** computer science departmental seminars during their course of study. These seminars are announced via email and on the department website.

5.  **Written Core Course Exam (Non-Thesis Option):** All non-thesis students must pass a departmental written core course exam.
    *   This exam covers the fundamental concepts from **CS 5329: Algorithm Design and Analysis**.
    *   The exam is typically taken after a student has completed at least 18 graduate credit hours.

**Note:** Failure to successfully complete these requirements within the timelines specified by the department and The Graduate College may result in dismissal from the program. Always consult with the graduate advisor to plan your milestones accordingly.
